Tax File Number (TFN) Guide

Everything you need to know about getting your Australian TFN

Last updated: 27 May 2025

Important: Without a TFN, employers will withhold tax at 45% from your wages. Apply as soon as you arrive in Australia!

üìã What is a Tax File Number (TFN)?

A Tax File Number (TFN) is your unique identifier for the Australian tax system. Think of it as your tax ID that follows you throughout your working life in Australia.

Why You Need a TFN:

  • Identifies you to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
  • Prevents 45% tax withholding on your wages
  • Required to open superannuation accounts
  • Needed to lodge tax returns
  • Allows you to earn bank interest without extra tax

üí° Good to Know

Your TFN is yours for life - even if you leave Australia and return later, you'll use the same number. Keep it secure and never share it publicly!

üñ•Ô∏è Application Process

The entire online application takes about 10 minutes. Follow these steps:

  1. Visit the ATO Website
    Go to ATO TFN Application
    Select "Foreign passport holder" form
  2. Enter Visa Details
    Input your visa subclass (417 or 462) when prompted
  3. Fill Identity Information
    Enter details exactly as they appear in your passport
  4. Provide Contact Details
    Enter your current Australian address and contact information
  5. Submit Application
    Review all information and submit - you'll receive an application receipt number
  6. Save Your Receipt
    Keep your receipt number safe - you'll need it if you start work before receiving your TFN

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost?

The TFN application is completely FREE. Be wary of any websites charging for this service.

What if I lose my TFN letter?

Create a myGov account and link it to the ATO. You can view your TFN online anytime once linked.

I'm changing address - what should I do?

Update your address via myGov or call the ATO on 13 28 61. This ensures any tax correspondence reaches you.

Leaving Australia early?

No need to cancel your TFN. Keep it for lodging your tax return and claiming superannuation refunds. The TFN remains valid if you return to Australia.

Can I work in multiple jobs with one TFN?

Yes! Use the same TFN for all employers. Just complete a new Tax File Number Declaration form for each job.
